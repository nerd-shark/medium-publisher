## Post Content

🤖 THE AI THAT "REPLACED" A DEPARTMENT

47 people. 12,000 claims per week. A backlog growing by 800 claims every week.

They built a multi-agent AI system. Four specialized agents working together.

Here's what actually happened 👇

---

📊 THE NUMBERS

Before → After:
• Team size: 47 → 19
• Processing time: 3.2 hours → 4.2 minutes
• Accuracy: 96.3%
• Annual savings: $2M
• Payback period: 10.8 months
• Workload automated: 83%

---

💰 THE COST TABLE (Infographic concept)

What the vendor promised: 70% cost reduction
What they actually got: 35% cost reduction

Why the gap?
• Monitoring infrastructure
• Retraining pipelines
• Human oversight (17% edge cases)
• Change management
• New work: reviewing AI decisions

Still saved $2M/year. Still transformative. Just not magical.

---

⚠️ WHAT BROKE

1. Hallucinations slipped past grounding on edge cases
2. Model drift: 3% accuracy drop over 6 weeks — undetected
3. Senior experts quit (jobs got "boring")
4. Trust calibration failure: humans either blindly trusted or constantly second-guessed

---

🏗️ THE ARCHITECTURE

Four specialized agents + one orchestrator:
🔹 Document Ingestion Agent
🔹 Coverage Analysis Agent
🔹 Damage Assessment Agent
🔹 Compliance Agent
🔹 Orchestrator (Promise/Work pattern)

Each agent does one thing well. The orchestrator routes claims based on confidence scores.

---

💡 THE HONEST TAKE

"Replaced a department" makes a better headline than reality.

They didn't replace the department. They restructured it. 19 people now handle the cases that require judgment, empathy, and ambiguity — the things AI still can't do.

Agentic AI is transformative. It's not magic.
