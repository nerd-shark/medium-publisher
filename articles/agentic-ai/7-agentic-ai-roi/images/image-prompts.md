# Featured Image Prompts — Agentic AI ROI

## Prompt 1: The Spreadsheet Meeting

A conference room where a finance person and an engineer are sitting across from each other at a table. Between them, a laptop is open showing a spreadsheet with cost columns and a bar chart. The finance person is pointing at a specific cell on the screen with a pen, eyebrows slightly raised — the "show me the numbers" expression. The engineer has a second laptop open showing a chatbot interface or agent dashboard, but they're looking at the spreadsheet, not their own screen. On the table: printed reports, a calculator, coffee cups. A whiteboard behind them has "Cost per Unit" written at the top with a partially visible breakdown. The mood is constructive tension — not hostile, but the engineer is being asked to justify the investment. Style: photorealistic, editorial photography, warm office lighting. 16:9 landscape. No readable text except the whiteboard header.

## Prompt 2: The Two Stacks

A split composition. Left side: a small, neat stack of coins (representing the API cost everyone sees) sitting on a desk, well-lit, clean. Right side: a much larger, messier pile of coins, receipts, invoices, and crumpled papers (representing the full cost stack — infrastructure, engineering, human review) partially hidden in shadow behind the first stack. A hand is pulling the small stack aside to reveal the larger pile behind it. The visual metaphor: the visible cost is a fraction of the real cost. Style: photorealistic, studio lighting, shallow depth of field. The contrast between the neat small stack and the messy large pile tells the story. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 3: The Pilot Graveyard

A dimly lit storage room or server closet with several monitors and laptops on shelves, all powered off, screens dark. Each has a sticky note on it — different colors, some curling at the edges. One monitor in the foreground has a faint glow, still running, with a dashboard visible showing green metrics. The rest are dead projects. A single overhead light illuminates the running monitor. The mood is quiet — these were all promising pilots once. Style: photorealistic, moody lighting, slight desaturation except for the one running monitor. 16:9 landscape. No readable text on sticky notes.

## Prompt 4: The Volume Dial

A close-up of a large industrial volume dial or knob (like a mixing board fader or a factory control dial) labeled from 0 to 50,000. The dial is currently set to about 1,000. Next to the dial, a small digital display shows a cost figure in red (high cost per unit). A hand is reaching for the dial to turn it up. The implication: volume is the variable that makes or breaks the economics. Industrial/control room aesthetic. Style: photorealistic, dramatic side lighting, shallow depth of field on the dial. 16:9 landscape. No readable numbers (just the visual of low-setting + red display).

## Prompt 5: The Break-Even Line

A large whiteboard in a conference room showing a hand-drawn graph. X-axis is volume (interactions per month), Y-axis is cost per unit. Two lines are drawn: a flat horizontal line (human cost per unit) and a steep curve that drops sharply as volume increases (agent cost per unit). The lines cross at a point circled in red marker — the break-even point. To the left of the crossing: the agent costs more. To the right: the agent wins. Someone has drawn an arrow pointing to the right side with "ROI zone" written next to it. Markers and an eraser sit on the whiteboard ledge. Style: photorealistic, the whiteboard drawing is rough and hand-drawn (not computer-generated), warm office lighting. 16:9 landscape. Minimal text (just "ROI zone" and axis labels).

---

## Usage Notes
- All prompts 16:9 landscape for Medium featured images
- Grounded in real settings — conference rooms, offices, control rooms
- No abstract metaphors or glowing digital visualizations
- Recommended for featured image: Prompt 1 (Spreadsheet Meeting) or Prompt 5 (Break-Even Line)
  - Prompt 1 captures the article's core tension: engineer meets finance
  - Prompt 5 is the article's thesis in one visual: volume determines ROI
- Prompt 2 (Two Stacks) would work well as a secondary image for the cost stack section
