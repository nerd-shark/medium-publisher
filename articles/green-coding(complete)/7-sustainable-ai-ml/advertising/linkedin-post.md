Training GPT-3 emitted 552 tons of CO₂. That's like driving 5 cars for their entire lifetime. 🚗🚗🚗🚗🚗

We're in an AI boom. Every company is training models. ChatGPT serves millions of requests per day. Each request burns compute. Each training run burns more.

Here's what nobody talks about: AI has a massive carbon problem.

🔥 GPT-3 training: $4.6M, thousands of GPUs, weeks of runtime
⚡ Inference at scale: 100 GPUs running 24/7 = 262,800 kWh/year
🔄 The retraining problem: most teams retrain on schedule, not when needed

But it doesn't have to be this way. You can train and deploy ML models efficiently:

**Model Efficiency**:
• Pruning: 90% smaller models, 95% accuracy retained
• Quantization: FP32 → INT8 = 4x speedup, 1-2% accuracy loss
• Knowledge distillation: DistilBERT is 40% smaller, 60% faster, 97% performance

**Training Optimization**:
• Mixed precision: 2x faster with 3 lines of code
• Early stopping: save 20-40% of training time
• Efficient batch sizes: find the sweet spot

**Carbon-Aware Training**:
• Schedule during low-carbon hours (Microsoft: 16% reduction)
• Use renewable-heavy regions (Stockholm: 13 gCO₂/kWh vs Virginia: 400 gCO₂/kWh)
• Pause/resume based on grid intensity

**Inference Optimization**:
• Batch inference: 10-100x more efficient than single-item
• Dynamic batching: 10ms latency for 10-100x throughput
• Model caching: 80% cache hit rate = 80% less compute

Real example: Team was retraining weekly. Model was stable for months. Switched to performance-based retraining. 80% reduction in retraining compute.

The tradeoff: 95% accuracy with 10x efficiency is often better than 98% accuracy with 1x efficiency.
