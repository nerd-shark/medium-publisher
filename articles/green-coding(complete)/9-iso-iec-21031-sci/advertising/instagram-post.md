Nine articles on making software more energy efficient. Algorithms, databases, CI/CD, ML training, language choices, microservices, carbon-aware computing.

All of it reduces carbon, but none of it gave me a single number I could actually track over time.

ISO/IEC 21031:2024 — the Software Carbon Intensity standard — changes that. One equation:

SCI = (E × I + M) per R

E is energy. I is how dirty the grid is. M is the carbon cost of manufacturing the hardware. R is your functional unit — per API call, per transaction, whatever matters to your software.

It's a rate, not a total. And offsets are explicitly excluded. You can't buy your way to a better score.

Accenture measured a production app at 0.025 gCO₂ per API call. Autostrade per l'Italia measured 60 apps and averaged 15.1% CO₂ savings. Some fixes took 4 person-days per app.

When I ran the calculation on my own service, embodied emissions were barely 2% of the score. E × I dominated the score. That changed where I focused.

The article walks through calculating your first SCI score with working code, real case studies, and the honest limitations.
