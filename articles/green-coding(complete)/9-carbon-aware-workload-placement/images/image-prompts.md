# Featured Image Prompts — Carbon-Aware Workload Placement

## Prompt 1: The Global Grid Map
A dramatic top-down view of a stylized world map made of circuit board patterns and glowing data lines. Different regions pulse with different colors representing carbon intensity — Scandinavia and France glow bright green (clean energy), Central Europe and parts of Asia glow amber to red (fossil fuels). Glowing data packets (represented as small light orbs) are visibly routing away from red regions toward green regions, leaving light trails. Cloud data center icons sit at key locations, some surrounded by green halos, others by red. A large arrow of light sweeps from a red zone to a green zone, representing workload migration. Style: dark background, satellite-view perspective, glowing neon data visualization aesthetic. 16:9 landscape. No text, no country labels.

## Prompt 2: The Time-Shift Clock
A large, futuristic circular clock face dominates the image. Instead of hours, the clock shows a gradient from dark red (high carbon, peak hours) through amber to bright green (low carbon, off-peak). The clock hands point to the green zone. Around the clock, batch processing jobs (represented as glowing geometric cubes) are queued up, waiting for the hands to reach the green zone before launching. Some cubes in the green zone are actively being processed with energy beams. A sun and moon icon on opposite sides of the clock show the day/night cycle. Solar panel and wind turbine silhouettes appear in the green zones. Style: clean, modern, infographic-inspired with glowing elements on dark background. 16:9 landscape. No text, no numbers on clock.

## Prompt 3: The Carbon Intensity Spectrum
A wide panoramic illustration showing a landscape that transitions from left to right. On the far left: a coal power plant with dark smoke, red-tinted sky, servers glowing hot red — high carbon intensity. In the middle: a natural gas plant, amber sky, servers glowing amber. On the far right: wind turbines and solar panels under a clear blue-green sky, servers glowing cool green — low carbon intensity. A stream of glowing data packets flows from left to right, migrating toward the clean energy side. The same computation (represented by identical glowing cubes) appears at each stage, showing the same work produces different carbon at different locations. Style: panoramic landscape, gradient transition, environmental contrast. 16:9 landscape. No text.

---

## Usage Notes
- All prompts 16:9 landscape for Medium featured images
- No text, no labels, no country names
- Focus on: geographic variation, time-shifting, clean vs dirty energy, data migration
- Color coding: green = clean/low carbon, red = dirty/high carbon, amber = medium
- Recommended: Prompt 1 (Global Grid Map) for featured image — most visually striking
