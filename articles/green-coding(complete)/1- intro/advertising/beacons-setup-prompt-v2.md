## Pro Tips

1. **Keep it updated**: Nothing worse than "Coming Soon" links that never come
2. **Use emojis**: Makes links more scannable and engaging
3. **Short descriptions**: 1-2 sentences max per link
4. **Mobile-first**: Most Instagram users are on mobile
5. **Track clicks**: Use Beacons analytics to see what resonates
6. **Consistent branding**: Match your Instagram aesthetic
7. **Test on mobile**: Always preview before publishing
8. **Series strategy**: Start with 1-2 series, expand as you publish more
9. **Reorder sections**: Put most active series at top
10. **Cross-promote**: Mention other series at end of each article
11. **Platform-specific content**: Tailor posts for each social platform
12. **Cross-link platforms**: Mention your other social accounts in posts
13. **Consistent username**: Use same handle across platforms when possible
14. **Profile optimization**: Complete all profile fields on each platform
15. **Engagement**: Respond to comments on all platforms

## Social Media Platform Strategy

**LinkedIn** (Professional audience):

- Focus on: Technical depth, career advice, industry insights
- Best for: Green Coding, Architecture, DevOps, Leadership series
- Post frequency: 2-3x per week
- Engagement: Comment on others' posts, join groups

**Medium** (Primary content platform):

- Focus on: Long-form articles, all series
- Best for: All content
- Post frequency: 1-2 articles per week
- Engagement: Respond to comments, clap for others

**X/Twitter** (Tech community):

- Focus on: Quick insights, threads, hot takes
- Best for: All series, bite-sized tips
- Post frequency: Daily
- Engagement: Reply to others, retweet, quote tweet

**Instagram** (Visual, younger audience):

- Focus on: Career tips, quick wins, behind-the-scenes
- Best for: Simplified versions of all series
- Post frequency: 3-5x per week
- Engagement: Stories, reels, carousel posts

**Threads** (Conversational):

- Focus on: Discussions, opinions, community building
- Best for: All series, more casual tone
- Post frequency: Daily
- Engagement: Reply to threads, start conversations

**Facebook** (Broad audience):

- Focus on: Detailed posts, community building
- Best for: All series, especially beginner-friendly content
- Post frequency: 2-3x per week
- Engagement: Join groups, participate in discussions

**GitHub** (Code-focused):

- Focus on: Code examples, open source contributions
- Best for: Green Coding, Architecture, DevOps series
- Post frequency: As you create repos/examples
- Engagement: Contribute to projects, review PRs

**Reddit** (Community-driven):

- Focus on: Detailed discussions, answering questions
- Best for: All series, especially technical deep dives
- Post frequency: 1-2x per week
- Engagement: Comment on others' posts, answer questions
