## Thread Version (Optional - For Extended Engagement)

**What is a Thread?**
A thread is a series of connected tweets that tell a longer story. You post the first tweet, then reply to it with the second tweet, reply to that with the third, etc. This allows you to share more detailed content while keeping each individual tweet under 280 characters.

**Why Use Threads?**
- **More engagement**: Each tweet in the thread can be liked/retweeted separately
- **Better storytelling**: Break complex topics into digestible chunks
- **Algorithm boost**: X's algorithm favors threads (more impressions)
- **Higher retention**: People are more likely to read 6 short tweets than 1 long article

**How to Post a Thread:**
1. Post Tweet 1/6 (the hook)
2. Click "Reply" on your own tweet
3. Post Tweet 2/6 (continue the story)
4. Repeat until you've posted all 6 tweets
5. The tweets will be connected with a line showing they're part of a thread

**When to Use Thread vs Single Tweet:**
- **Single tweet**: Quick announcement, driving traffic to article
- **Thread**: Want to provide value on X itself, build engagement, establish expertise

**Pro Tips:**
- Number your tweets (1/6, 2/6, etc.) so people know it's a thread
- Start with a hook (Tweet 1) that makes people want to read more
- End with a call-to-action (Tweet 6) linking to your full article
- Post all tweets within 5-10 minutes to keep momentum
- Pin the first tweet to your profile for visibility
