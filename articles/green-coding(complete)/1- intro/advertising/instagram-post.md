## Caption

That API call you just made? Same carbon footprint as boiling water for tea ☕

Software isn't virtual. It runs on physical servers consuming massive amounts of electricity. Global data centers use 1-2% of worldwide electricity. If the internet were a country, it'd rank 6th in energy consumption.

Green coding = writing software that minimizes energy consumption and carbon emissions.

Why care?
💰 Energy efficiency = cost savings
⚡ Efficient code is better code
🌍 Small changes at scale = massive impact

Quick wins:
✅ Switch List to HashSet (100x faster, 40% less CPU)
✅ Delete unused cloud resources
✅ Optimize images (5MB → 500KB)
✅ Right-size your infrastructure
✅ Run workloads in low-carbon regions

Real talk: C is 75x more energy efficient than Python. But you don't need to rewrite everything—just be intentional about where efficiency matters.

Your code runs millions of times. Those small inefficiencies add up fast.

## WHAT "LINK IN BIO" MEANS

Instagram doesn't allow clickable links in post captions (except for verified accounts with 10K+ followers who can use link stickers in Stories).

**"Link in bio" means:**
1. You put the article URL in your Instagram profile bio section
2. In your post caption, you tell people "link in bio"
3. People click on your profile name → see your bio → click the link there

**How to add link to your Instagram bio:**
1. Go to your Instagram profile
2. Tap "Edit Profile"
3. In the "Website" field, paste: https://tinyurl.com/yr4txmvj
4. Tap "Done"

**Alternative: Use Linktree or Similar**

If you post multiple articles, use a link aggregator service:
- **Linktree** (free): Create one link that shows multiple article links
- **Beacons** (free): Similar to Linktree
- **Bio.link** (free): Another alternative

Then your bio link goes to a page with all your articles, and you update it each time you post.

**Pro Tip for This Post:**
Since you're starting a series, consider setting up Linktree now. Add this article as the first link, then add future articles as you publish them. Your bio stays the same ("Link to all my articles in bio") but the Linktree page updates.
