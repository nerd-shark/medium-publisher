## The Invisible Work Problem

Technical debt is one of the hardest things to communicate to non-technical stakeholders. They can't see it. They can't measure it. And when you say "we need to refactor," they hear "the engineers want to rewrite code that already works instead of building features customers are asking for."

## What Actually Works

The article covers practical techniques for making technical debt visible and building the business case for addressing it:

- **The interest payment metaphor** — "We're spending 30% of every sprint on workarounds. That's the interest payment on our technical debt."
- **Quantifying the cost**: velocity trends, bug rate increases, incident frequency, developer time on workarounds
- **Visualizing the problem** — SonarQube metrics, heat maps, trend charts that non-technical people can actually read
- "6 weeks of refactoring saves 2 hours per developer per week. With 8 developers, that's 16 hours/week. Payback in 12 weeks." That's the **ROI calculation** that gets budget approved.

The key insight: technical debt is a business risk, and it needs business language to get addressed.
