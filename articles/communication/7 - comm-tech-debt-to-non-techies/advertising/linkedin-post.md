Ever seen a high-performing engineering team suddenly grind to a halt? Same people, same hours, but velocity drops 40%? 📉

That's what happened at a fintech company I consulted. New features went from 3 days to 2 weeks. The VP kept asking, "Why so slow?" Engineers begged for headcount, but the real culprit was technical debt.

The breakthrough? 💡 Stop talking code. Start talking money. 💰

"Remember that payment shortcut that saved us 2 weeks? It's cost us $84K in extra time since—interest on a $20K loan."

Stakeholders get it instantly when you frame debt as financial debt.

Back it with data: Velocity fell from 45 to 28 story points/sprint. Bugs doubled. Incidents quadrupled.

Don't ask to "refactor." Ask for velocity investment: "6 weeks, $120K, recovers $200K/year in capacity."

Propose 20% of each sprint for debt paydown. Features keep shipping, progress happens.
