## Thread

**2/6**
The metaphor that changed everything for me: interest payments.

"We took a shortcut 6 months ago. Saved 2 weeks. Since then, every payment feature costs 3 extra days. 14 features × 3 days = $84K in engineering time.

We're paying $84K interest on a $20K loan."

VP got it immediately.

**3/6**
Metaphors open the door. Numbers close the deal.

Pull from YOUR tools, not industry benchmarks:

Velocity: 45 pts/sprint → 28 (plot the trendline)
Bugs: 3/feature → 7 (each costs ~6 hours)
Incidents: 2/month → 8 ($12K revenue impact each)

Real data from your own systems hits different.

**4/6**
Don't ask for permission to refactor. Ask for investment in velocity.

"6-week investment: $120K
Return: 40% faster delivery, 60% fewer bugs
Recovered capacity: $200K/year
Payback: 4 months"

Frame it as ROI. That's a language they already speak.

**5/6**
The 20% allocation is the sweet spot.

20% of each sprint for debt paydown.

Small enough: features keep shipping, nobody panics
Big enough: real progress, measurable improvement
Sustainable: you can do this indefinitely

Promise measurable results in 6 weeks. Then deliver.

**6/6**
And celebrate wins loudly. This is the part most engineers skip.

"Refactored payment module. Delivery time: 14 days → 5 days. Bugs: 7/feature → 2."

Visible results → more trust → more time → better velocity → repeat.
