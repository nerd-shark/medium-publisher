# Featured Image Prompts — The HealthCare.gov Rescue Capstone

## Prompt 1: The Oval Office Briefing (RECOMMENDED)
A cinematic over-the-shoulder shot from behind a seated President Obama in the Oval Office. We see the back of his head and shoulders — he's leaning slightly forward, listening intently. Across from him, standing near a small easel or holding a single-page brief, is a confident engineering leader in business casual (no suit jacket, rolled sleeves) — clearly a tech person, not a politician — calmly and deliberately walking through an emergency recovery plan. The engineer's posture is composed, authoritative, unhurried. On the coffee table between them: a laptop open to a dashboard, a printed one-pager with a simple chart. Two or three other team members sit on the couches flanking the engineer, notebooks open. The iconic Oval Office details are unmistakable — the Resolute desk in the soft background, the curved walls, the presidential seal on the carpet, the tall windows with gold drapes letting in late-afternoon light. The mood is serious but controlled — not panic, but focused urgency. Warm, natural lighting. Photorealistic. 16:9 landscape. No text, no labels, no UI overlays.

## Prompt 2: The 60-Day Timeline
A dramatic horizontal timeline stretching across the frame. Left side: chaos — a crumbling digital structure (representing the failed website) surrounded by red alert symbols, political lightning bolts, and scattered disconnected nodes. Center: a team of figures arriving, establishing communication lines (glowing connections between previously disconnected nodes). Right side: a rebuilt, functioning structure with green status indicators and flowing data streams. Above the timeline, eight small icons represent the eight communication techniques, each lighting up at different points along the journey. Style: clean, infographic-inspired, dark background. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 3: The Translation Layer
A central figure standing between two worlds. On the left: a technical world of code, servers, databases, and architecture diagrams (blue-green glow). On the right: a political/executive world of podiums, briefing rooms, documents, and decision tables (gold glow). The central figure acts as a translator — receiving information from one side and transforming it (visible transformation effect) before passing it to the other. Both sides are connected through the translator. Style: symbolic, split-composition, dark background. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 4: The Eight Bridges
Eight bridges of different colors spanning a dark chasm between two cliff faces. One cliff represents "technical excellence" (circuit patterns, code), the other represents "organizational impact" (buildings, people, outcomes). Each bridge is a different communication technique, and together they form a complete crossing. Some bridges are wider (more critical), some are narrower, but all are necessary. A figure walks across the bridges. Style: dramatic, architectural, dark void between cliffs. 16:9 landscape. No text.

---

## Usage Notes
- All prompts 16:9 landscape for Medium featured images
- No text, no labels
- Recommended: **Prompt 1 (Oval Office Briefing)** for featured image — grounds the article in the real-world gravity of the crisis, shows the core thesis (an engineer communicating to the most powerful audience imaginable), and is immediately recognizable and compelling as a thumbnail
- Prompt 1 note: The back-of-head framing keeps the focus on the engineer and the moment, not on generating a presidential likeness — this is about the communicator, not the politician
- Remaining prompts kept as alternates if a more abstract/symbolic style is preferred
