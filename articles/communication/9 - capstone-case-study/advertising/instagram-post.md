## Caption

$840 million. 55 contractors. 6 enrollments on launch day. 💀

The HealthCare.gov rescue is the most dramatic case study in technical communication ever.

A small team had 60 days to fix a system that nobody fully understood, while briefing the White House, navigating contractor politics, and working under congressional scrutiny.

Every communication technique in one crisis:
🎯 Executive briefings (Pyramid Principle)
🤝 Stakeholder navigation (neutral facilitation)
🎤 Mixed-audience presentations (daily war rooms)
🏗️ Architecture reviews (reverse-engineering 55 contractors' work)
📋 Triage facilitation (200+ critical bugs)
📝 Technical writing for government
💰 Technical debt advocacy to political staff
🌉 Cross-functional collaboration (Silicon Valley meets government)

Result: 50,000 concurrent users (up from 1,100). 800K+ enrollments in December. The team became the US Digital Service.

The engineers who saved it weren't just technically excellent. They were communicators.
