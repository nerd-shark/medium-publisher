## Thread Version

🧵 The HealthCare.gov rescue is the best case study in technical communication ever produced. Here's how one team used every communication skill in 60 days.

1/ Oct 1, 2013. HealthCare.gov launches. $840M spent. 4.7M visitors. 6 enrollments. SIX. Congressional hearings within days. A small team of engineers from Google, Oracle, Red Hat arrives in Maryland with 60 days to fix it.

2/ SPEAKING EXECUTIVE: Briefing the President. "The database connection pool is exhausted" → "The site handles 1,100 users. We need 50,000. We can get there by Dec 1 if we get these 3 things." Pyramid Principle. "So What?" test. Every update.

3/ STAKEHOLDER DYNAMICS: CMS protecting contracts. Contractors protecting blame. White House wanting numbers. Congress wanting accountability. The rescue team stayed neutral, focused on the system, and aligned stakeholders offline before joint meetings.

4/ CROSS-FUNCTIONAL: Silicon Valley engineers (deploy daily) + government contractors (quarterly releases). Different cultures, tools, processes. Shared metric: enrollment numbers. "Yes, and your code has issues AND here's how we fix it together."

5/ TECHNICAL DEBT: The whole system was debt. "Every day we don't fix the database layer costs us 2,000 enrollments." That sentence moved political staff. "The ORM generates N+1 queries" did not.

6/ Result: Dec 1 — 50,000 concurrent users (up from 1,100). 800K+ enrollments in December. The team became the US Digital Service. Communication made the technical fixes possible.
