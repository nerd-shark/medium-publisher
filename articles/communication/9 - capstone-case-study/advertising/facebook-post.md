$840 million. 55 contractors. 6 enrollments on launch day.

The HealthCare.gov rescue in late 2013 is the most dramatic real-world case study in technical communication I've ever encountered. A small team of engineers had 60 days to fix a catastrophically broken system while briefing the White House, navigating contractor politics, and working under congressional scrutiny.

Every communication technique from my 9-part series was used in this single crisis — executive briefings, stakeholder navigation, mixed-audience presentations, architecture reviews, triage facilitation, technical writing for government, technical debt advocacy, and cross-functional collaboration between Silicon Valley and the federal government.

By December 1: 50,000 concurrent users (up from 1,100). 800,000+ enrollments. The team became the US Digital Service.

This is the capstone of my Communication series — showing that communication carried as much weight as the technical work itself.
