# LinkedIn Post - Design Review Playbook

## Post

Your design review just turned into a 2-hour MongoDB vs PostgreSQL debate. No decisions made. Everyone's frustrated. Sound familiar?

Here's what went wrong: Nobody was facilitating. Everyone was advocating.

I've facilitated 100+ design reviews across fintech, healthcare, and e-commerce. The pattern is clear: design reviews fail when there's no facilitator—just presenters and advocates.

In my latest article, I break down the facilitator's playbook:

🎯 The 4 types of design reviews (Requirements, Preliminary, Detailed, Final) and when to use each
🛑 The 5 deadly patterns that kill design reviews (bikeshedding, loud voices, analysis paralysis, design by committee, missing context)
⚖️ How to stay neutral when facilitating disagreements between senior engineers
📋 Decision frameworks that actually work (consensus vs consent vs DACI)
✅ The 90-minute microservices review that made 3 decisions and documented everything

Key insight: Facilitating is fundamentally different from presenting. You're guiding the process, not advocating for an outcome.

The hardest scenario? When you're BOTH presenter and facilitator. I show you how to explicitly separate the roles.

Whether you're a tech lead running your first design review or an engineering manager trying to make reviews more productive, this playbook gives you practical techniques that work.
