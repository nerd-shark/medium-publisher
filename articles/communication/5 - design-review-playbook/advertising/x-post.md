## Thread Version

1/ Design reviews fail when there's no facilitator, only advocates.

Your 2-hour meeting ends with "let's schedule a follow-up." No decisions. No progress. Just $1,800 wasted.

Here's the playbook I use after facilitating 100+ design reviews:

2/ First, understand the difference:

Presenter = Advocate for your solution
Facilitator = Guide the process, stay neutral

Most people try to do both. That's the problem.

3/ The 4 types of design reviews:

• Requirements DR: Validate the problem (60-90 min)
• Preliminary DR: Validate high-level approach (90-120 min)
• Detailed DR: Validate implementation details (2-3 hours)
• Final DR: Validate production readiness (60-90 min)

High-risk projects need all 4. Low-risk projects need 1.

4/ The 5 deadly patterns:

1. Bikeshedding: 45 min on variable naming, 5 min on database choice
2. Loud voices: Principal engineer dominates, juniors stay quiet
3. Analysis paralysis: "Let's evaluate 5 more options"
4. Design by committee: Everyone compromises, nobody's happy
5. Missing context: Reviewers suggest things already tried and failed

5/ How to facilitate disagreements:

• Stay neutral (even if you have an opinion)
• Structure the debate: "Let's hear both perspectives fully"
• Find common ground: "What do we agree on?"
• Focus on tradeoffs, not right vs wrong
• Know when to escalate

6/ Decision frameworks:

Consensus = Everyone agrees (rare, slow)
Consent = No blocking objections (practical)
DACI = Clear roles (Driver, Approver, Contributors, Informed)

Most design reviews work best with consent.

7/ The hardest scenario: You're BOTH presenter and facilitator.

Solution: Explicitly separate the roles.

"I'm in facilitator mode now. I want to hear all concerns."

Then switch back: "Let me switch to presenter mode to explain why..."

8/ Document everything in ADRs (Architecture Decision Records):

• What was decided
• Why (the reasoning)
• Alternatives rejected
• Consequences (good and bad)

Send follow-up within 24 hours or decisions evaporate.

9/ Real example: 90-minute microservices review

• Made 3 decisions (service boundaries, distributed transactions, migration strategy)
• Everyone contributed (including junior engineer)
• Documented in ADRs within 24 hours

That's what good facilitation looks like.

10/ Key takeaway:

Facilitating ≠ Presenting

You're guiding the process, not advocating for an outcome.
