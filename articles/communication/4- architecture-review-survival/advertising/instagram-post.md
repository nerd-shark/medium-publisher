THE FIVE QUESTIONS EVERY ARCHITECTURE REVIEW BOARD IS REALLY ASKING 🎯

🔹 Will this scale? (Not "does it work now?" but "will it work at 10x scale?")
🔹 What happens when it fails? (Everything fails eventually)
🔹 Can we actually operate this? (Brilliant but unmaintainable = bad design)
🔹 Does this fit our architecture? (Are you creating a special snowflake?)
🔹 What are the alternatives? (Did you actually think this through?)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE 60-MINUTE REVIEW STRUCTURE ⏱️

5 min: Context and decision needed
10 min: Architecture overview
10 min: Key decisions and rationale
10 min: Scalability & reliability
20 min: Q&A and deep dives
5 min: Wrap-up and next steps

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHAT TO PREPARE 📄

25-35 page architecture document
Send 3 days before review
Include: diagrams, scalability analysis, security, operations, alternatives, risks

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

HANDLING PUSHBACK ✅

"This is over-engineered" → Show complexity is justified
"This won't scale" → Show the math
"Doesn't fit our architecture" → Explain your choices
"Too risky" → Acknowledge risk, show mitigation

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE META-SKILL: ARCHITECTURAL HUMILITY 🙏

The best architects aren't the ones with perfect designs. They're the ones who acknowledge uncertainty, invite scrutiny, and adapt based on feedback.

Architecture reviews aren't about proving your design is perfect. They're about proving you've thought through the implications.
