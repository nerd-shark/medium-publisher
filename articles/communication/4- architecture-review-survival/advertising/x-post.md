The 5 questions every architecture review board is really asking:

🔹 Will this scale?
🔹 What happens when it fails?
🔹 Can we actually operate this?
🔹 Does this fit our architecture?
🔹 What are the alternatives?

**Tweet 3/8**
Architecture reviews test whether you've thought through implications, not whether your design is perfect.

Senior architects have seen systems succeed and fail. Their job is to catch problems before they become 3 AM pages.

**Tweet 4/8**
The 60-minute review structure:

5 min: Context and decision needed
10 min: Architecture overview
10 min: Key decisions and rationale
10 min: Scalability & reliability
20 min: Q&A and deep dives
5 min: Wrap-up and next steps

**Tweet 5/8**
What to prepare (mandatory):

📄 25-35 page architecture document
📄 Send 3 days before review
📄 Include: diagrams, scalability analysis, security, operations, alternatives considered, risks

**Tweet 6/8**
Handling pushback:

"This is over-engineered" → Show complexity is justified
"This won't scale" → Show the math
"Doesn't fit our architecture" → Explain where you followed standards
"Too risky" → Acknowledge risk, show mitigation

**Tweet 7/8**
Anti-patterns to avoid:

❌ The Mystery Box (too vague)
❌ The Perfect Design (no tradeoffs)
❌ The Technology Resume (listing tools without why)
❌ The Defensive Stance
❌ The Handwave ("we'll figure it out later")

**Tweet 8/8**
The meta-skill: Architectural humility.

The best architects acknowledge uncertainty, invite scrutiny, and adapt based on feedback.
