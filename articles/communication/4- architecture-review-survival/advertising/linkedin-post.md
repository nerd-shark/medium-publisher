You're 20 minutes into your architecture review. You've presented your microservices design, explained your database choices, walked through your scaling strategy.

Then the Principal Architect leans forward: "Why are you using event sourcing here? That's going to make debugging a nightmare."

The VP of Engineering: "How does this handle European data residency requirements?"

The Security Architect: "I don't see encryption at rest. Are we storing PII in plaintext?"

All eyes are on you. This isn't a presentation anymore. It's an interrogation.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE FIVE QUESTIONS EVERY REVIEW BOARD ASKS

🔹 Will this scale? (Not "does it work now?" but "will it work at 10x?")
🔹 What happens when it fails? (Everything fails eventually)
🔹 Can we operate this? (Brilliant but unmaintainable = bad)
🔹 Does this fit our architecture? (Are you creating a snowflake?)
🔹 What are the alternatives? (Did you think this through?)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE 60-MINUTE ARCHITECTURE REVIEW STRUCTURE

🎯 Opening (5 min): Context and decision needed
🎯 Architecture Overview (10 min): High-level system design
🎯 Key Decisions (10 min): 3-5 most important choices and why
🎯 Scalability & Reliability (10 min): Show the math, prove it scales
🎯 Q&A (20 min): Deep dives into specific concerns
🎯 Wrap-Up (5 min): Summary and next steps

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHAT TO PREPARE (MANDATORY)

📄 Architecture document (25-35 pages)
📄 Send 3 days before review
📄 Include: context, requirements, diagrams, scalability analysis, security, operations, alternatives, risks

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

HANDLING PUSHBACK

✅ "This is over-engineered" → Show complexity is justified by real requirements
✅ "This won't scale" → Show the math and load test results
✅ "Doesn't fit our architecture" → Explain where you followed standards and why you diverged
✅ "What about [alternative]?" → Show tradeoff analysis
✅ "This is too risky" → Acknowledge risk, show mitigation, demonstrate testing

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ARCHITECTURE REVIEW ANTI-PATTERNS TO AVOID

❌ The Mystery Box (too vague)
❌ The Perfect Design (no tradeoffs acknowledged)
❌ The Technology Resume (listing tools without explaining why)
❌ The Defensive Stance (getting defensive when questioned)
❌ The Handwave ("we'll figure it out during implementation")

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE META-SKILL: ARCHITECTURAL HUMILITY

The best architects aren't the ones with perfect designs. They're the ones who acknowledge uncertainty, invite scrutiny, and adapt based on feedback.

Architecture reviews aren't about proving your design is perfect. They're about proving you've thought through the implications.
