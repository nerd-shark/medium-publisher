# Gemini Image Prompts for Architecture Review Survival Guide

## Article Theme
The article focuses on surviving architecture review boards by preparing your thinking rather than just your slides. Key themes include: defending technical decisions, handling interrogation-style questioning, understanding tradeoffs, architectural humility, and the five core concerns (scale, failure, operations, fit, alternatives).

## Prompt 1: The Architecture Review Interrogation Scene

Create a professional, modern illustration showing an architecture review meeting scene. In the foreground, show a confident software architect standing at a presentation screen displaying system architecture diagrams with microservices, databases, and cloud infrastructure icons. Around a conference table, show 4-5 senior architects and executives leaning forward with engaged, questioning expressions. The atmosphere should feel intense but professional - like a PhD defense rather than hostile. Use a color palette of deep blues, grays, and accent colors (orange or teal). The style should be clean, geometric, and slightly abstract - think modern tech company aesthetic. Include subtle visual elements suggesting preparation: the presenter has notes, diagrams show tradeoff matrices, and there's a sense of thoughtful deliberation rather than panic. Lighting should emphasize the presenter as confident and prepared.

## Prompt 2: The Five Questions Concept Visualization

Create an abstract, conceptual illustration representing the five core architecture concerns as interconnected pillars or nodes. Visualize: 1) "Scale" as expanding/growing geometric shapes, 2) "Failure" as circuit breakers or safety nets, 3) "Operations" as gears or monitoring dashboards, 4) "Fit" as puzzle pieces connecting, and 5) "Alternatives" as branching paths or decision trees. In the center, show a system architecture diagram (microservices, databases, APIs) being evaluated through these five lenses. Use a modern, tech-forward color scheme with gradients (blues, purples, teals). The style should be clean, geometric, and slightly futuristic - suggesting systematic thinking and thorough analysis. Include subtle connecting lines or flows between the five concerns showing they're interconnected. The overall feel should be "prepared thinking" and "systematic evaluation."

## Prompt 3: The Tradeoff Matrix Concept

Create a sophisticated, data-visualization style illustration showing architectural decision-making as a balance or matrix. Visualize a central system architecture (cloud infrastructure, microservices, databases) surrounded by competing forces or considerations shown as balanced scales, comparison charts, or matrix grids. Show visual representations of tradeoffs: complexity vs simplicity, scalability vs operational overhead, consistency vs availability, synchronous vs asynchronous. Use a professional color palette of navy blue, slate gray, with accent colors (orange, teal, or green) for highlights. The style should be clean, modern, and slightly abstract - like a high-end technical infographic. Include subtle elements suggesting preparation and analysis: checkmarks, comparison arrows, decision nodes. The overall composition should convey "thoughtful tradeoff analysis" and "informed decision-making" rather than perfect solutions.

## Design Guidelines

**Color Palette Suggestions**:
- Primary: Deep blue (#1a365d), Slate gray (#475569)
- Accents: Teal (#14b8a6), Orange (#f97316), or Purple (#8b5cf6)
- Background: Light gray (#f8fafc) or white

**Style Notes**:
- Modern, clean, geometric aesthetic
- Avoid overly realistic or photographic styles
- Prefer abstract/conceptual over literal representations
- Professional tech company aesthetic (think Stripe, Vercel, or modern SaaS)
- Should work well as a Medium featured image (1200x630px or similar)

**Mood**:
- Confident but humble
- Prepared and thoughtful
- Professional and serious but not intimidating
- Emphasis on systematic thinking and preparation

## Usage Notes

These prompts are designed for Gemini image generation. Each prompt emphasizes different aspects of the article:

1. **Prompt 1**: Focuses on the human element - the review meeting itself
2. **Prompt 2**: Focuses on the conceptual framework - the five questions
3. **Prompt 3**: Focuses on the methodology - tradeoff analysis

Choose the prompt that best represents the article's core message or generate all three and select the strongest result.
