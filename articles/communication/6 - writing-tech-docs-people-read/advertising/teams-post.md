## One Document, Three Audiences, Three Reading Styles

Executives skim — title, first paragraph, conclusion. 30 seconds. Product managers cherry-pick — impact, timeline, dependencies. They skip implementation entirely. Engineers read everything and want more.

Most of us write the whole document for that third audience and wonder why nobody else reads it.

## The Fix: Layers, Not Separate Documents

**Layer 1** — Executive summary. 1 page, 300 words max. Problem, solution, cost, ask. Written last, placed first.

**Layer 2** — Technical overview. 3-5 pages. Architecture, decisions, tradeoffs. For your engineering director.

**Layer 3** — Implementation details. Appendix. Unlimited. For the engineers who'll build it.

Each layer stands on its own. Executives stop at Layer 1. PMs stop at Layer 2. Engineers read Layer 3. Nobody wades through content that isn't for them.

The real test: hand page 1 to a non-technical colleague. If they can't explain what you're proposing and why — rewrite it.
