📄 You spent 3 weeks on a 47-page RFC.

Two people read it. One skimmed the conclusion.

The project got approved based on a hallway conversation.

The problem isn't your technical depth. It's your document structure.

---

Three audiences, three reading patterns:

Executives: 30 seconds. Title, first paragraph, done.
Product managers: Impact and timeline. Skip implementation.
Engineers: Everything. Want more.

One document written for engineers. Read by everyone. That's why it fails.

---

The fix: layered documents. Executive summary on top (1 page, 300 words). Technical overview in the middle. Implementation in the appendix.

Each layer self-contained. Readers stop at their level.

Answer first. Evidence second. Context third.
