**Subject**: Medium article series — sustainable engineering, resilience, AI agents, and more

Hey everyone,

I've been writing on Medium as a side project for the past few months — started with one article on green coding and it kind of snowballed into multiple series. I'm posting summaries to the Thought Leadership channel in Teams, but wanted to give a broader heads-up since some of these might be useful for your teams.

**What's out there now:**

**Green Coding / Sustainable Engineering** (9 parts — complete)
Covers carbon-aware applications, energy-efficient algorithms, sustainable microservices, green DevOps, language efficiency benchmarks, and carbon-aware workload placement. If your team runs anything on the cloud, there are some quick wins in here.

**Resilience Engineering** (8 parts published, 4 more coming)
Cell-based architecture, chaos engineering, observability, graceful degradation, multi-region disaster recovery, rate limiting, the bulkhead pattern, and incident response. Practical patterns with real production examples — the kind of stuff that saves you at 3 AM.

**Technical Communication** (7 parts published, 2 more coming — wrapping up soon)
Executive communication, stakeholder dynamics, architecture reviews, design reviews, technical writing, communicating technical debt, and cross-functional collaboration. The series closes with a case study of the HealthCare.gov rescue that ties all the techniques together. If you've ever struggled to explain a technical decision to a non-technical audience, this series is for that.

**Agentic AI** (6 parts published, 2 more coming)
From chatbots to autonomous agents — multi-agent systems, the Promise/Work orchestration pattern, memory systems, and building your first agentic system. Aimed at engineers and architects thinking about where AI agents fit in production systems.

**Coming soon:**

**The Quantum-Centric Architect** (starts April — 12 parts)
Quantum computing from the architect's chair, not the physicist's whiteboard. Post-quantum cryptography migration, hybrid quantum-classical patterns, the cryptographic bill of materials, and what "quantum-ready" actually means. If your systems use RSA or ECC (they do), the Q-Day timeline is worth understanding.

**U.S. AI Policy** (starts late April — 12 parts)
AI governance, workforce displacement, reskilling policy, deterrence stability, and what the regulatory landscape actually looks like. Less technical, more strategic — aimed at leaders thinking about how AI policy affects their organizations and teams.
