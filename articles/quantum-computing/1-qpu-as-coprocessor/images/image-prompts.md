# Featured Image Prompts — The QPU as a Coprocessor

## Prompt 1: The Chandelier

A dramatic photograph-style shot looking up at a real dilution refrigerator — the gold-plated "chandelier" of tubes, cables, and cooling stages that houses a quantum processor. The refrigerator hangs from the ceiling of a clean room, surrounded by cables and control electronics. The lighting is dramatic — cool blue-white from the clean room lights, with warm gold reflections off the refrigerator's plating. A single engineer in a clean room suit stands at the base, looking up at it, providing scale — the refrigerator is about 10 feet tall. The mood is awe mixed with engineering pragmatism — this is impressive hardware, but it's also just infrastructure. Style: photorealistic, dramatic upward angle, clean room environment. Cool and warm light contrast. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 2: The Coprocessor Rack

A data center rack shot from a slight angle. The rack contains familiar hardware — standard servers with blinking LEDs, network switches, cable management. But in the middle of the rack, one unit is different: a sleek, angular module with a distinctive blue-violet glow and frost crystals forming on its surface — the quantum coprocessor. Cables connect it to the classical servers above and below. The contrast is the point: the QPU sits alongside the classical hardware, not replacing it. A small status display on the quantum module shows "READY" with a temperature reading. Style: photorealistic, data center lighting (cool overhead fluorescent + LED indicators), shallow depth of field focused on the quantum module. 16:9 landscape. No readable text except the status display.

## Prompt 3: The GPU Parallel

A split composition showing two eras side by side. Left side: a circa-2012 server room where someone is installing a GPU card into a rack server — the early days of GPU computing for ML. The hardware is recognizable, the setting is slightly dated. Right side: a modern clean room where someone is connecting a quantum module to a control rack — the early days of QPU computing. The two scenes mirror each other in composition — same action (installing specialized hardware), different eras. The visual parallel between GPU adoption and QPU adoption is the point. Style: photorealistic, the left side slightly warmer/more dated in color grading, the right side cooler/more modern. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 4: The Job Queue

A control room with multiple monitors showing quantum job management dashboards. One large central monitor shows a job queue — several jobs listed with status indicators (queued, running, completed). A smaller monitor shows a quantum circuit diagram. Another shows measurement results as a histogram (bar chart of bitstring probabilities). An operator sits at the console, coffee in hand, watching the queue. The setting is mundane and operational — this isn't a physics lab, it's an ops center. The mood is routine: quantum computing as infrastructure, not as science fiction. Style: photorealistic, control room lighting (dim ambient + bright monitors), the operator is relaxed and professional. 16:9 landscape. No readable text on monitors.

## Prompt 5: The Cloud API Call

A developer's desk with a laptop showing a code editor (Python/Qiskit code visible but not readable). On the screen, a terminal window shows a job submission to a cloud quantum service — a progress indicator and a "Job ID" returned. Next to the laptop, a second monitor shows a cloud provider dashboard (IBM Quantum or Amazon Braket style) with a world map showing QPU locations as glowing dots. The developer's hands are on the keyboard. Coffee, headphones, and a notebook with hand-drawn circuit diagrams sit on the desk. The mood is practical — this is a developer using a cloud API, not a physicist in a lab. Style: photorealistic, overhead flat-lay or slight angle, warm desk lamp lighting. 16:9 landscape. No readable code or text.

## Usage Notes
- All prompts 16:9 landscape for Medium featured images
- Grounded in real settings — clean rooms, data centers, developer desks, control rooms
- The through-line: quantum computing as infrastructure, not science fiction
- Recommended for featured image: Prompt 1 (Chandelier) or Prompt 2 (Coprocessor Rack)
  - Prompt 1 is the most visually striking — the dilution refrigerator is iconic and immediately recognizable as "quantum"
  - Prompt 2 is more aligned with the article's thesis — the QPU sitting alongside classical hardware in a normal rack
  - Prompt 3 (GPU Parallel) is the strongest conceptual image but harder to execute well in generation
- Prompt 4 (Job Queue) and Prompt 5 (Cloud API Call) work as secondary images for the access models section
