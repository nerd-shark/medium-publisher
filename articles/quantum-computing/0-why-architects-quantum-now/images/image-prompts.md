# Featured Image Prompts — Why Architects Need to Think About Quantum Now

## Prompt 1: The Encryption Expiration Clock
A dramatic visualization of a massive digital vault door made of glowing cryptographic symbols (RSA, ECC, key icons) with a large countdown clock embedded in its center. The clock shows a date range "2029-2035" in amber warning colors. Behind the vault door, streams of encrypted data glow blue-green. In the shadows beyond the vault, faint quantum circuit patterns pulse with an ominous violet glow, approaching the door. Cracks of violet light are beginning to appear at the edges of the vault. Style: cinematic, dark background, cybersecurity aesthetic with quantum elements. 16:9 landscape. No text, no labels.

## Prompt 2: The Harvest Now, Decrypt Later
A split-scene illustration. Left side: streams of encrypted data (blue-green glowing packets) flowing through fiber optic cables, being intercepted and diverted into massive dark storage silos by shadowy figures. The silos are labeled with quantum circuit patterns. Right side: a future timeline showing the same silos opening, the data now readable, glowing red with exposed secrets. A quantum computer (abstract, glowing violet crystalline structure) sits between the two scenes, connected by a timeline arrow. Style: technical-thriller aesthetic, dark background, split composition. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 3: The Quantum Landscape Map
A bird's-eye view of a futuristic technology landscape showing different "islands" or "territories" representing quantum computing providers. Each island has a distinct architectural style: IBM (large, industrial, many interconnected buildings), Google (sleek, modern, with a glowing breakthrough beacon), IonQ (precise, crystalline structures), Amazon (a bridge connecting multiple islands). Between the islands, data streams flow. In the center, a compass rose shows "Architecture Decisions" as the cardinal direction. Style: clean, map-like, isometric perspective, dark ocean background with glowing islands. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 4: The Migration Timeline
A dramatic highway stretching into the distance, transitioning from classical computing (blue circuit board patterns on the road surface) to quantum computing (violet quantum circuit patterns). Road signs along the highway mark milestones: NIST standards, Chrome PQC, Q-Day horizon. The road is well-lit and clearly marked — the path is defined. Behind the viewer, the road is already traveled. Ahead, the road is clear but the horizon glows with quantum violet. Style: cinematic, perspective shot, technology-highway metaphor. 16:9 landscape. No text, no readable labels.
