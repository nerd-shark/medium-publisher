Someone is probably storing your encrypted traffic right now, waiting for a quantum computer that can read it.

That's not speculation. CISA estimates over 50% of nation-state actors are running "harvest now, decrypt later" operations — intercepting TLS sessions, VPN tunnels, and API calls today, banking on quantum hardware catching up within the decade.

NIST finalized post-quantum cryptography standards in August 2024. Chrome ships hybrid PQC by default. The migration path exists. But most architecture teams I talk to haven't started a cryptographic inventory, let alone a migration plan.

I wrote a practical guide for architects. It covers what actually breaks on Q-Day (and what doesn't), what a Cryptographic Bill of Materials looks like, and the cost math that gets CFO attention — $1-2M migration vs. $50-500M exposure. There are five things you can start Monday morning.
