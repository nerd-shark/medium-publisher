## Post Content

🚨 Black Friday 2018. Walmart.com goes down.

3.6 million shoppers affected. ~$9M in lost sales.

The root cause? The system never learned to say "no."

Traffic spiked → users refreshed → mobile apps retried → load tripled → everything crashed.

Healthy at 10:00 AM. Dead by 10:03 AM.

---

The fix isn't more servers. Auto-scaling takes minutes. The cascade takes seconds.

The fix is rate limiting and backpressure — teaching your system to push back.

Amazon disables recommendations during Prime Day to protect checkout. Generic pages, but people can still buy things.

---

Your system's job isn't to accept every request. It's to serve as many as it can, well, and gracefully decline the rest.

That's not failure. That's resilience.
