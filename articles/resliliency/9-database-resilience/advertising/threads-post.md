Your database primary just died. You have 30 seconds before customers notice.

The counterintuitive insight: high-traffic systems fail faster during database incidents. More requests per second = faster connection pool exhaustion. Your busiest services are your most fragile.

At 100 req/s, pool exhausts in ~25 seconds. At 500 req/s? Under 10 seconds.

Part 9 of my Resilience Engineering series covers what actually kills apps during DB failures — and it's usually not the database itself. It's the connection pool.
