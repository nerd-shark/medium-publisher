# Featured Image Prompts — Incident Response

## Prompt 1: The War Room Clock
A dramatic overhead view of a futuristic incident war room. A large circular countdown timer dominates the center of the room, showing "00:10:00" in glowing amber. Around it, holographic screens display system dashboards — some green, some flashing red. Three distinct workstations are visible: one for coordination (gold glow), one for technical investigation (blue glow), one for communications (green glow). The room is dark except for the screens and the central clock. A sense of urgency without chaos. Style: cinematic, dark, command-center aesthetic. 16:9 landscape. No text, no readable labels.

## Prompt 2: The 3 AM Phone Call
A dark bedroom scene lit only by the harsh glow of a phone screen showing a critical alert notification (red pulsing icon). A laptop on the nightstand is opening, its screen casting blue light. In the background, through a window, a city skyline with one building's lights flickering — representing the failing system. The contrast between the peaceful dark room and the urgent glow of the devices creates tension. Style: cinematic, moody, realistic-digital hybrid. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 3: The Incident Timeline
A horizontal timeline visualization stretching across the frame. Left side (red zone): chaos — scattered alert icons, question marks, no coordination. A clock shows "18 minutes wasted." Center (amber transition): roles being assigned, represented by three distinct colored nodes connecting. Right side (green zone): coordinated response — parallel investigation streams, clear communication lines, mitigation actions. The visual progression from chaos to coordination is the focal point. Style: clean, infographic-inspired, dark background with glowing elements. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 4: The Runbook
A dramatic close-up of a glowing digital runbook floating in a dark space, its pages showing step-by-step procedures with checkboxes and decision trees. Around it, the chaos of an incident — red alert particles, system error symbols — swirls but doesn't touch the runbook. The runbook is a calm island of order in the storm. A hand reaches toward it. Style: dramatic, dark background, the runbook as a beacon of clarity. 16:9 landscape. No text, no readable content.

---

## Usage Notes
- All prompts 16:9 landscape for Medium featured images
- No text, no labels
- Color coding: red = alert/failure, amber = urgency/transition, green = resolution/healthy, gold = coordination
- Recommended: Prompt 1 (War Room Clock) for featured image — captures urgency and coordination
