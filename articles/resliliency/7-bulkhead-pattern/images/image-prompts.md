# Featured Image Prompts — The Bulkhead Pattern

## Prompt 1: The Ship Cross-Section
A dramatic cross-section illustration of a futuristic digital ship made of circuit board patterns and glowing data streams. The ship is divided into 5 clearly visible watertight compartments separated by thick glowing bulkhead walls. One compartment (center-left) has a breach — red warning lights, water (represented as chaotic red data particles) flooding in. But the bulkhead walls hold firm — the adjacent compartments glow healthy blue-green with data flowing normally. Small repair bots work in the damaged compartment. The contrast between the one flooded compartment and the four healthy ones is the focal point. A status display above the ship shows "4/5 compartments operational." Style: technical cross-section, blueprint aesthetic with glowing neon elements. Dark ocean-like background. 16:9 landscape. No text, no labels.

## Prompt 2: The Isolated Thread Pools
A top-down view of a futuristic factory floor with 4 distinct, physically separated work areas, each enclosed by glowing barrier walls of different colors. Area 1 (gold, largest): "checkout" — robots working smoothly, green status lights. Area 2 (blue, medium): "search" — robots working smoothly. Area 3 (red, small): "recommendations" — robots are jammed, red warning lights, smoke, chaos. Area 4 (gray, small): "other" — robots working normally. The key visual: the chaos in Area 3 is completely contained by its barrier walls. The other three areas are unaffected. Overhead, a monitoring display shows resource utilization bars for each area. Style: clean, industrial-modern, top-down perspective. Dark background with glowing barriers. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 3: The Resource Isolation Diagram
An architectural illustration showing a central server node splitting into 4 separate resource channels, each with its own pipeline and capacity gauge. The top channel (gold, thick pipe) flows to a "critical path" destination — gauge shows 50% utilization, green. The second channel (blue, medium pipe) flows to "search" — gauge shows 40%, green. The third channel (red, thin pipe) flows to "external API" — gauge shows 100%, red, with overflow being rejected (particles bouncing off a barrier). The fourth channel (gray, thin pipe) flows to "other" — gauge shows 30%, green. The critical insight: the red channel at 100% doesn't affect the other channels at all. Each has its own independent capacity. Style: clean, technical, flow-diagram aesthetic with glowing pipes and gauges. Dark background. 16:9 landscape. No text.

## Prompt 4: The Wartime Ship Bulkhead
A dramatic cutaway of a WWII-era battleship or ocean liner with a torpedo strike breaching one compartment on the starboard side. The damaged compartment floods with dark red water/data particles, while the surrounding compartments remain dry and glowing blue-green behind intact bulkhead walls. Visible steel bulkheads are thick and glowing, clearly holding the damage inside. The rest of the ship continues sailing steadily through a stormy sea. Style: cinematic historical-technical, dark ocean background, 16:9 landscape. No text, no labels.

---

## Usage Notes
- All prompts 16:9 landscape for Medium featured images
- No text, no labels
- Focus on: isolation, containment, independent failure, resource separation
- Color coding: gold = critical/protected, red = failing/overloaded, blue/green = healthy
- Recommended: Prompt 1 (Ship Cross-Section) for featured image — strongest visual metaphor
